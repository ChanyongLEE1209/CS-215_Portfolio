Mini Project 5: Working with Geospatial Data

For this project, I analyzed Professor Wirfs-Brock's location data from her visit to Whitman College in March 2022. I used latitude, longitude, timestamps, and the haversine formula to study how her movement changed over time.

Hypothesis

My hypothesis is that Professor Wirfs-Brock's movement followed a travel pattern: long-distance arrival, a concentrated stay near Whitman College, and long-distance departure.

Interactive Map

The map shows several major location clusters, including Seattle, Whitman College in Walla Walla, one point along the travel route, and Denver. This suggests that the data captures both local campus movement and long-distance travel.

Distance from Reid Campus Center Over Time

To support my hypothesis, I calculated Professor Wirfs-Brock's distance from Reid Campus Center at each timestamp. The graph shows that the distance starts very high, drops close to zero during the Whitman visit, and then rises again near the end of the dataset.

Conclusion

The visualizations support my hypothesis. Professor Wirfs-Brock appears to have traveled from a distant location, spent most of the visit near Whitman College, and then departed to another distant location. The map shows the spatial pattern, while the distance graph shows the movement pattern over time.
